About

Welcome to my ultimate documentary and biography collection—a handpicked mix of the fascinating, the eye-opening, and the downright jaw-dropping! Now, don’t get me wrong, I love a good serial killer story as much as the next person (and trust me, there are plenty out there), but I decided to give them a pass this time and focus on some other gems. Documentaries

Grim Realities: True Crime & Unsolved Mysteries

These mind-bending documentaries delve into real-life crimes, investigations, and unsolved cases, often with twists and turns that keep you guessing.

  • The Tinder Swindler (2022)

    • This documentary follows the shocking story of Simon Leviev, a conman who used the popular dating app Tinder to scam women out of millions of dollars. He posed as a wealthy businessman, showering his victims with lavish gifts before manipulating them into lending him large sums of money, under the guise of danger and deceit. The film showcases the experiences of the women who were deceived and their journey to uncover the truth and seek justice.

  • The Murdaugh Murders (2022)

    • This documentary investigates the mysterious deaths of Maggie and Paul Murdaugh, members of a powerful South Carolina family, and the alleged involvement of Alex Murdaugh, their patriarch. It uncovers a tangled web of deceit, financial fraud, and the unraveling of a once-prominent family’s legacy.

  • Making a Murderer (2015)

    • A true crime documentary series that chronicles the life of Steven Avery, a man who was wrongfully convicted of a crime, exonerated, and then accused of a new crime. The series explores the flawed criminal justice system and the possibility of Avery’s innocence in the murder of Teresa Halbach, leading to national debate.

  • Evil Genius

    • This documentary follows the bizarre and chilling story of the pizza bomber case, where a bank robber, Brian Wells, was forced to commit a crime while wearing a bomb attached to his neck. The film delves into the mystery of who orchestrated this twisted plot and the shocking secrets that unraveled in its aftermath.

  • Lover, Stalker, Killer (2021)

    • A documentary series exploring a collection of chilling real-life stories where intense romantic relationships turn deadly. Each episode focuses on an individual case where love, obsession, and stalking ultimately lead to murder.

Fighting for Truth: Social Injustices & Human Rights

These focus on wrongful convictions, the fight for justice, and the powerful stories of people seeking the truth. These are a few of my eye-opening favorite documentaries.

  • The Innocence Files (2020)

    • This documentary series explores the work of the Innocence Project, an organization dedicated to exonerating wrongfully convicted individuals. The series examines several cases where DNA evidence was used to prove the innocence of those imprisoned, shining a light on flaws in the criminal justice system and the devastating consequences of wrongful convictions.

  • Innocence Project (2020)

    • This documentary investigates the mysterious deaths of Maggie and Paul Murdaugh, members of a powerful South Carolina family, and the alleged involvement of Alex Murdaugh, their patriarch. It uncovers a tangled web of deceit, financial fraud, and the unraveling of a once-prominent family’s legacy.

  • Trial 4 (2020)

    • This docuseries follows the case of Sean K. Ellis, a man who was wrongfully convicted of the 1993 murder of a Boston police officer. It highlights his decades-long fight for justice as he spends years in prison while seeking to prove his innocence. The series also delves into the issues of racial injustice and corruption within the legal system.

  • Keep Sweet: Pray and Obey (2022)

    • This documentary series delves into the Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints (FLDS) and its controversial leader Warren Jeffs. It follows the experiences of women and girls who were subjected to the oppressive and abusive practices within the church, including polygamy and forced marriages, and their attempts to escape.

  • The Six Triple Eight (2022)

    • This documentary focuses on the 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion, the only all-Black, all-female unit to serve overseas during World War II. Stationed in France, they worked tirelessly to sort and deliver backlogged mail to soldiers, overcoming racial and gender barriers to provide vital support to the war effort.

Stars & Scandals: Famous Faces & Untold Stories

This section captures the lives, struggles, and successes of famous people, from celebrities to sports icons. Here’s a list of a few of my favorite personal reflection documentaries.

  • Whitney (2018)

    • This documentary, Whitney, delves into the life of the legendary Whitney Houston, exploring her rise to fame, struggles with addiction, and her complicated personal life. Through rare archival footage, personal interviews, and intimate details, the film paints a portrait of one of the most iconic and troubled stars in music history.

  • Bobby Brown: Every Little Step (2022)

    • This documentary, Bobby Brown: Every Little Step, follows the life and career of Bobby Brown, one of R&B’s biggest stars in the 1980s and 1990s. It explores his music career, tumultuous personal life, struggles with addiction, and the impact of his relationship with Whitney Houston. The film provides a deep look into his journey of redemption.

  • Mickie’s Big Break (2021)

    • Mickie’s Big Break (or Mickie Thompson: The Racing Story) follows Mickie Thompson, a race car driver who, along with his son, is determined to build some of the fastest race cars in the world. The documentary highlights his passion for racing, the trials and triumphs he faces, and his family’s dedication to pushing the limits of the sport.

  • The Curious Case of Natalia Grace (2022)

    • A documentary series that investigates the disturbing story of Natalia Grace, a young Ukrainian girl who was adopted by an American family and later accused of being an adult posing as a child. The film examines the controversy surrounding her age and the tragic events that unfolded within the family.

  • The Act (2019)

    • The Act is a dramatized miniseries based on the real-life story of Gypsy Rose Blanchard. In 2015, Gypsy, who had been subjected to years of abuse by her mother, Dee Dee Blanchard, became involved in her mother’s murder. The series explores the complex relationship between Gypsy and Dee Dee, exposing a disturbing case of Munchausen syndrome by proxy.

Nature’s Wonders: Transformation & Discovery

These explore personal transformations, discoveries, or the profound relationships between humans and nature. Here are a few of my favorite documentaries on the emotional beauty of nature’s wonders.

  • My Octopus Teacher (2020)

    • This is a heartwarming and visually stunning documentary that follows filmmaker Craig Foster as he forms an extraordinary bond with a wild octopus in the kelp forests off the coast of South Africa. The film explores the beauty of the underwater world, as well as themes of connection, empathy, and the healing power of nature.

  • The Mysterious History of Cattle Mutilation (2020)

    • A documentary that delves into the bizarre and unexplained phenomenon of cattle mutilations, which have been occurring for decades across various parts of the world. The film examines the theories behind these strange occurrences, from UFOs to secret government experiments, and investigates the mystery surrounding these gruesome events.

  • Seaspiracy (2021)

    • A documentary that uncovers the hidden truths about the global fishing industry and its devastating impact on the environment. Directed by Ali Tabrizi, the film explores the harmful practices of overfishing, the destruction of marine ecosystems, and the ethical dilemmas surrounding sustainable seafood, urging viewers to reconsider their relationship with the oceans.
